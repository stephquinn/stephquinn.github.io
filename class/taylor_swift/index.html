Taylor Swift:
The Master of the Product Launch

Originally published by Cision.com

From the intentionality of each lyric and note, to the precision with which she applies her red lipstick, Taylor Swift leaves no details to chance.

Ms. Swift made an indelible mark on the global stage as a singer-songwriter with extraordinary talent and has built a business on her own terms. While her art is one of communication — through words and notes and human emotions — Taylor Swift should also be recognized as a masterful communicator in the digital age while off-stage.

At its core, the field of communications is one of persuasion — whether one is selling an idea, a product or a service — and Taylor Swift does this like few others. Using the launch of her 10th original studio album Midnights as an exemplar, a playbook emerges with best practices for a successful launch.

Though some paid promotions were activated in the countdown to the release of Midnights, Taylor Swift relied heavily on earned, shared and owned channels for her well-planned communications strategy.

Given her status as a multiple Grammy Award–winner and global icon, without a well-planned launch campaign....

  • Would she have been the first recording artist to capture slots 1 through 10 on the Billboard Singles chart at the same time? Probably.
  • Would Midnights have gone double-platinum without each well-timed post and public appearance? We think so.
  • Would Taylor Swift have been named the most-streamed female artist on Spotify by the Guinness Book of World Records? All signs point to yes.
